A **volume tier** triggers a deeper discount when the buyer's cart quantity for a product reaches a threshold. Use volume tiers to encourage bulk purchasing.

## How a volume tier works

A volume tier has:

- A **product** (or **variant**) it applies to.
- A **minimum quantity** — the threshold that triggers the discount.
- A **discount type** (`percentage` or `fixed amount`) and **value**.

You can stack multiple tiers on the same product:

| Quantity | Discount |
| --- | --- |
| 10 | 15% off |
| 50 | 25% off |
| 100 | 30% off |

When the cart hits each threshold, the deeper tier replaces the shallower one. Tiers don't compound.

## Quantity is summed per product

Volume tiers count quantity **across all variants of the same product**. If the buyer adds:

- 30 of *T-shirt — Red / Large*
- 25 of *T-shirt — Blue / Medium*

…the product total is 55. A *50+* tier on *T-shirt* triggers, and **both lines** get the deeper discount.

This matches how wholesale buyers think — they order *55 T-shirts*, not *55 of one SKU*.

## Create a volume tier

1. Go to **Pricing**, then click the group you want to add a tier to.
2. In the **Volume tiers** card, click **Add tier**.
3. Choose the scope:
   - **Product** — counts quantity across all variants.
   - **Variant** — counts only that variant's quantity.
4. Pick the product (or variant).
5. Enter the **minimum quantity**, **discount type**, and **value**.
6. Click **Save**.

To add more thresholds for the same product, click **Add tier** again with a higher minimum quantity.

## Resolution rules

For each cart line, the app:

1. Sums the quantity per product (across variants) and per variant (where variant-level tiers exist).
2. Picks the highest-threshold tier whose minimum is met.
3. Compares that tier's price to the group baseline and any product/variant price rules.
4. Applies the **lowest final price**.

This means a volume tier can be "beaten" by a price rule, or vice versa — you don't need to coordinate them manually.

## Edit or delete a tier

- **Edit** — click the tier's row to change the minimum quantity, type, or value.
- **Delete** — click the trash icon.

Changes apply within seconds at checkout via the Shopify Function and on product pages via the theme block.

## Tips

- Start with **two or three tiers** per product. Buyers find more than three thresholds confusing.
- Use **product-level** tiers for most cases. Variant-level tiers are useful only when one variant has a very different cost basis.
- Combine with the **MOQ** (minimum order quantity) setting to require a baseline quantity before checkout.

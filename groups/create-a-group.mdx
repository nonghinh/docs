title: Create a wholesale group
description: Walk through the new-group form, including discount type, priority, and auto-tagging.
---

Creating a group takes about a minute. After saving, the group is immediately available — but it has no customers and no pricing rules until you add them.

## Steps

1. From the dashboard, go to **Groups** in the left navigation.
2. Click **Create group**.
3. Fill in the form:

   | Field | Notes |
   | --- | --- |
   | **Name** | Customer-facing label (for example, *Silver tier*). |
   | **Slug** | Auto-generated from the name. Edit only if you have a reason — the slug becomes part of the customer tag. |
   | **Discount type** | Choose **Percentage** or **Fixed amount**. |
   | **Discount value** | Enter `20` for 20%, or `10` for $10. |
   | **Priority** | Leave at `0` unless you operate overlapping groups. Higher numbers win ties. |
   | **Auto-tag customers** | Keep this **on**. The app writes `wholesale-{slug}` to every customer assigned to the group. |
   | **Active** | Keep on. Toggle off to disable pricing without deleting the group. |

4. Click **Save**.

The group now appears in the **Groups** list with a customer count of `0`.

## What happens after saving

- A Shopify-compatible slug is reserved (the app rejects duplicate slugs).
- The group is added to the metafield synced to the checkout function.
- The group is selectable as the **Assign to group** target on registration forms.

## Edit or delete a group

- **Edit** — click the group's row and change any field. Renaming the group does **not** rename the slug; slug changes would orphan customer tags.
- **Delete** — only allowed when the group has zero customers. Move customers to another group first (see [Add customers](/groups/manage-customers)).

<Warning>
  Avoid changing the slug after a group has customers. The customer tag is tied to the slug, and renaming would leave existing customers tagged with a slug that no longer matches any group.
</Warning>

## Tips

- Start with **three tiers** — for example, *Silver / Gold / VIP* at 10% / 20% / 30%. You can refine later.
- Use **fixed-amount** discounts only when prospects expect a specific dollar amount (rare). Percentage discounts scale better as your catalog grows.
- Set **priority** explicitly only if you allow customers to belong to multiple groups (uncommon).

Five concepts cover almost everything the app does. Read this once and the rest of the docs will read more easily.

## Wholesale group

A **wholesale group** (sometimes called a *tier*) is a named bucket of customers that share the same baseline discount and pricing rules.

A group has:

- A **name** and **slug** — the slug is used in the customer tag.
- A **discount type** — `percentage` or `fixed`.
- A **discount value** — for example, `20` for 20% off, or `10` for $10 off.
- A **priority** — used to break ties when a customer somehow ends up in two groups.
- Optional **price rules** and **volume tiers** that override the baseline.

→ [Wholesale groups](/groups)

## Customer tag

A customer becomes "wholesale" by carrying a tag in the format:

```
wholesale-{group_slug}
```

For example, a customer in the **Silver** group has the tag `wholesale-silver`.

The tag is the bridge between the app and Shopify checkout. The app writes the tag automatically when you assign a customer to a group; the checkout discount function reads the tag at checkout time to decide which prices apply.

<Warning>
  Don't manually edit `wholesale-*` tags in Shopify Admin. Always assign customers from the **Customers** page in the app — otherwise the tag and the database can drift out of sync.
</Warning>

## Price rule

A **price rule** overrides a group's baseline discount for a specific product or variant. For example, the **Silver** group might get 20% off everything *except* one premium product where it gets 10% off.

→ [Price rules](/pricing/price-rules)

## Volume tier

A **volume tier** triggers a deeper discount when the cart quantity for a product reaches a threshold. For example, *buy 50 or more, get 30% off*.

Quantities are summed **across variants of the same product**. If a buyer adds 30 of variant A and 25 of variant B, the product quantity is 55.

→ [Volume tiers](/pricing/volume-tiers)

## Best price wins

When multiple rules could apply to the same cart line — say, a group baseline of 20% plus a product price rule of 25% plus a volume tier at 30% — the app picks **the lowest final price** for the customer. You don't need to disable rules to prevent overlap.

→ [Pricing model overview](/pricing)

## How a discount reaches checkout

<Steps>
  <Step title="You configure pricing in the app">
    Define groups, price rules, and volume tiers from the merchant admin.
  </Step>
  <Step title="The app syncs to a metafield">
    Your configuration is written to a shop-level metafield within seconds.
  </Step>
  <Step title="The Shopify Function reads at checkout">
    The discount function inspects the buyer's `wholesale-*` tag and the metafield, then computes the line-level discount.
  </Step>
  <Step title="The buyer sees wholesale prices">
    Discounts appear in the cart and at checkout — no theme code or workflow automation required.
  </Step>
</Steps>
